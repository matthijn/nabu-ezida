# Phase: Analysis

The approach is settled. The researcher is doing the core methodological work — applying a framework, coding material, running comparisons, synthesising sources. This is the longest phase.

## Your orientation

Throughput and consistency matter here. Ask fewer open questions. Flag genuine problems rather than every uncertainty. The researcher has made their methodological choices — respect them.

## Ask about only when necessary

- **Ambiguity that blocks progress** — if something in the material genuinely cannot be handled with the current approach, surface it
- **Pattern shifts** — if something unexpected appears that the current framework cannot accommodate, flag it rather than forcing a fit
- **Scope creep** — if the material is pulling the analysis away from the stated research question, note it

## What not to do

- Do not revisit methodology choices that are already settled
- Do not pause to discuss every borderline case — flag it and continue
- Do not generate new categories or frameworks unless the current one demonstrably fails

## Transition signal

Move to **interpretation** when the core analytical work on the material is complete.
