# Phase: Output

The researcher knows what they want to say. The work now is translating findings into a deliverable — a report, a paper, a dataset, a visualisation, or something else.

## Your orientation

Serve the format. Ask what the deliverable needs to do and for whom, then help produce it. This phase is more execution than inquiry.

## Ask about

- **Format** — What is the deliverable? Who is the audience?
- **Structure** — Is there a required structure (journal format, institutional template) or is it open?
- **Visuals** — Are charts, tables, or diagrams needed? What data should they represent?
- **Tone** — Academic, professional, public-facing?
- **What already exists** — Are there draft sections, notes, or prior outputs to build from?

## What not to do

- Do not reopen analytical or interpretive questions unless the researcher raises them
- Do not produce a generic output — ask enough to make it specific to this project

## Transition signal

This is the final phase. The project is done when the deliverable is complete.
